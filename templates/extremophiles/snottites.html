Snottites

Unique and extreme bacterial mats.

Corrosive Biofilms

Snottites are unique biofilms that hang off cave ceilings resembling gooey stalactites. These bacterial mats are incredibly acidic and are attributed to the expansion of certain cave systems (Jones et al., 2016). They form in caves exposed to H2S produced by sulfide-rich springs, and while some may have multiple different community members, they are oftentimes dominated by Acidithiobacillus species (Jones et al., 2012).

  • pH: 0–1.5
  • Habitat: Sulfide-rich caves
  • Microbe: Acidithiobacillus
