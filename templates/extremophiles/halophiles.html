Salt Tolerance

Halophiles

Microbes adapted to extreme salinity, from salt flats to hypersaline lakes.

Halobacterium salinarum: A salty diva.

Halobacterium salinarum is well known for its purple color, resulting from the production of bacteriorhodopsin. This protein is an important light-driven ion pump that helps drive the production of chemical energy. This pump has roused a fair amount of scientific intrigue given its simplicity, making it one of the best understood pumps of its kind. H. salinarum is energetically flexible, capable of growing as an aerobic chemoorganotroph or anaerobically relying on the bacteriorhodopsin pump. Interestingly, the proposed oxidative phosphorylation pathway has demonstrated differences from the traditional and better studied pathways in E. coli and the mitochondria. Furthermore, the genome itself has shown itself to be remarkably plastic with its large number of insertion elements, a feature thought to drive rapid adaptation to fluctuating saline environments.
